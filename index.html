Talking Heads was an american rock group from New York. They played a mix of punk, art-rock, world music and funk. Hailed as one of the most popular bands of the 80s, they influenced the music scene in New York a great deal.

Talking Heads was formed in 1975 and was active until 1991. The band consisted of David Byrne (vocals), Chris Frantz (drums), Tina Weymouth (bass) and Jerry Harrison (keybord, guitar). Talking Heads became a member of Rock 'n Roll Hall of fame in 2002.
